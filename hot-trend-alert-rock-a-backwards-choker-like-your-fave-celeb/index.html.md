# Hot Trend Alert! Rock a Backwards Choker like Your Fave Celeb ...

What do Emma Watson and Elizabeth Banks have in common (besides their complete and utter marvelousness)? Both have been spotted wearing backwards chokers, and if you love this chic trend, you can rock one, too!

![](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/bq/ew/lmkoz2j0_550x825.jpg)

Here's Elizabeth Banks (aka The Hunger Games' Effie Trinket) at the Mockingjay Part 1 premiere, wearing a minimalist backwards choker... gorgeous!

![](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fm/mq/pcfv5ins_600x717.jpg)

And Emma Watson at the 2014 BAFTA Los Angeles Jaguar Britannia Awards... again, completely chic and lovely!

Luckily, there's a backwards choker for every fashionista's budget...

## 1. Jennifer Fisher round Reverse Choker

![Jennifer Fisher round Reverse Choker](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/gl/w9/yipxqj80_600x387.jpg)

Choose gold or silver and wear to your office holiday party with an off-the-shoulder LBD and a sleek updo.

## 2. Caroline Mesh Choker

![Caroline Mesh Choker](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/x3/fn/ad38fxks_600x293.jpg)

Slightly more goth-inspired, still so versatile! I'd wear this to work with a crisp, tailored white button-down, pinstripe pants, and tall stilettos.

## 3. Flexible Rhinestone Choker

![Flexible Rhinestone Choker](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/eu/dg/u9oa6thv_600x229.jpg)

Add a little bling to the trend with this simple but sparkly choker. I'd wear it to add shine to any outfit, really. Because is there any outfit that couldn't use a little extra glam?

## 4. Kensie Season of White Choker

![Kensie Season of White Choker](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ul/af/xc77piv1_600x606.jpg)

[amazon.com](http://www.amazon.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)
Add this pearl-accented backwards choker to your most ladylike ensembles, or add feminine flair to an office appropriate menswear-inspired suit.

I can't wait to try this trend - who about you?
