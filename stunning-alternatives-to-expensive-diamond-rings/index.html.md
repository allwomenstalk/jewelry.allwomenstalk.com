# 8 Stunning Alternatives to Expensive Diamond Rings ...

When you think of an engagement ring, or any ring for that matter, one of the first things that will pop into your head is a classic white diamond ring. While you can’t go wrong with the classics, there are so many alternatives out there nowadays that it would be foolish to not consider other options.

Emeralds, sapphires, and even lab grown diamonds are experiencing a huge surge in popularity recently. [A ruby ring can be a great alternative](https://www.leibish.com/ruby-gemstones-rings) too. With the unprecedented availability of different gemstones today, you should have no problem finding the perfect alternative to that expensive diamond ring you’ve been dreaming of.

## 1. Sapphires: Beautiful, Customizable, and Sexy

One of the more popular diamond alternatives currently on the market is sapphire. These beautiful gemstones have been popular for thousands of years. Sapphires come in a variety of colors (blue, pink, green, red, clear, purple, and black), and can be cut into many different shapes. Like any other gemstone, their price is determined by their quality and color.

## 2. The Ruby - King of Gemstones

Rubies have been one of the most sought-after gemstones since the beginning of history. There’s a reason these stunning stones are known as “the king of gemstones.” Rubies vary in color from pink to the classic deep, dark red tone that they’re known for. An excellent option as an alternative to a diamond ring. [You can choose from Leibish’s ruby gemstones collection](https://www.leibish.com/ruby-gemstones) and find the perfect ruby ring today.

## 3. Emeralds

The emerald is one of the most popular gemstones in the world (and for good reason). Their regal, elegant color exudes a classic charm that makes them a very unique (yet timeless) option for rings. First discovered in ancient Egypt thousands of years ago, emeralds have been a favorite of queens, princesses, and everyday women ever since. Remember though, that emeralds are fairly soft (compared to diamonds, rubies, or sapphires), so be sure to choose an extra sturdy setting for your ring.

## 4. Lab Grown Diamonds

If you’ve been paying attention to the jewelry industry for the past few years, then you’ve undoubtedly heard about lab grown diamonds. So, what’s all the hype about then? Are they really better (or worse) than natural diamonds?

Besides being up to 20% less costly than natural diamonds, lab grown diamonds are guaranteed to be conflict-free. Technically, of course, they’re still “real” diamonds (they were just grown in a lab rather than mined from the earth). If you’re worried about the ethical implications of natural diamonds (and want to save some money while you’re at it), [lab grown diamonds](https://medium.com/lakediamond/the-high-tech-world-of-lab-grown-diamonds-1a15858a83de) are a solid alternative to consider.

## 5. Moissanite: the Gemstone from Space

Moissanite isn’t as well known as the other gemstones we’ve mentioned, but it’s a really cool alternative stone to think about purchasing. Although natural moissanite is very rare (it comes from space after all), lab grown variants are just as stunning and certainly lighter on your wallet. Moissanite that’s been cut well and set properly will look nearly identical to any white diamond.

## 6. Aquamarine

This beautiful semi-precious gemstone dates all the way back to ancient Greece. Aquamarine gained major popularity in the 17th century, and has been a favorite of high-end jewelry houses ever since. The stone’s color varies from pale blue/green tones to deep, dark blues. Symbolically speaking, aquamarine has been used to represent the ocean (which is where its name is derived from).

## 7. Amethyst

Amethyst has seen a major surge in popularity in recent years, being used in everything from earrings to engagement rings (and literally everywhere in between). This popular and elegant gemstone ranges in color from dark purple all the way to light pink. You can find amethysts cut in many different shapes/styles and nearly all of them have top-notch clarity ratings.

## 8. Pearls: Iconic and Timeless

Pearls have been sought after for their unique beauty _since we began wearing jewelry_. They’re unique, jaw-droppingly gorgeous, and sophisticated. The only drawback to using pearls as an alternative to diamonds, is that they’re relatively soft (and more prone to scratching). If you can see past their inherent fragileness, pearls are a very unique and refined option to consider.

Whether you’re looking for a diamond engagement ring alternative, or you’re just curious about what other gemstones are out there, you can’t go wrong with any of the options listed above. From timeless pearls to elegant emeralds, spacey moissanites to sexy sapphires - the options are endless!
